BAB 5

Pronoun and Noun

Learning about pronouns and nouns — the building blocks of English sentences. Understanding how to use them correctly in daily communication.

🏠 Beranda 📝 Uji Formatif Bab 5 ← Bab Sebelumnya Bab Selanjutnya →

A. Introduction to Pronouns and Nouns

Noun (kata benda) is a word used to name a person, place, thing, animal, or idea. Nouns are the building blocks of sentences.

Pronoun (kata ganti) is a word used to replace a noun to avoid repetition. For example, instead of saying "Siti is smart. Siti studies hard.", we can say "Siti is smart. She studies hard."

💡 Why are they important?
Nouns and pronouns help us communicate clearly and avoid repeating the same words over and over again.

B. Types of Nouns

1. Common Noun vs. Proper Noun

  • Common Noun: General name for a person, place, or thing (not capitalized).
    • Examples: teacher, city, book, school, student
  • Proper Noun: Specific name of a person, place, or thing (always capitalized).
    • Examples: Betti, Bukittinggi, English, MTsN 6 Agam, Monday

2. Concrete Noun vs. Abstract Noun

  • Concrete Noun: Things we can see, touch, hear, smell, or taste.
    • Examples: table, flower, music, perfume, coffee
  • Abstract Noun: Things we cannot see or touch (ideas, feelings, qualities).
    • Examples: love, happiness, honesty, knowledge, beauty

3. Countable Noun vs. Uncountable Noun

  • Countable Noun: Things we can count (have singular and plural forms).
    • Examples: book/books, pen/pens, student/students
  • Uncountable Noun: Things we cannot count (no plural form).
    • Examples: water, rice, sugar, information, advice

4. Collective Noun

A noun that refers to a group of people or things:

  • a flock of birds
  • a herd of cows
  • a class of students
  • a team of players
  • a bunch of flowers

C. Types of Pronouns

1. Personal Pronouns

Used to refer to specific people or things:

Person Subject Object Possessive Adjective Possessive Pronoun
1st (singular) I me my mine
2nd you you your yours
3rd (male) he him his his
3rd (female) she her her hers
3rd (thing/animal) it it its —
1st (plural) we us our ours
3rd (plural) they them their theirs

2. Demonstrative Pronouns

Used to point to specific things:

  • This (singular, near): This is my book.
  • That (singular, far): That is your bag.
  • These (plural, near): These are my friends.
  • Those (plural, far): Those are their houses.

3. Reflexive Pronouns

Used when the subject and object are the same person:

  • I hurt myself.
  • She taught herself English.
  • They enjoyed themselves at the party.
  • He cut himself while cooking.

4. Interrogative Pronouns

Used to ask questions:

  • Who (for people): Who is she?
  • What (for things): What is this?
  • Which (for choices): Which do you prefer?
  • Whom (formal, for people): Whom did you meet?
  • Whose (for possession): Whose book is this?

5. Indefinite Pronouns

Used to refer to non-specific people or things:

  • Someone, somebody, anyone, anybody
  • Something, anything, nothing
  • Everyone, everybody, no one, nobody
  • Each, every, all, some, many, few

D. Subject vs. Object Pronouns

Understanding when to use subject and object pronouns is very important:

  • Subject Pronoun: Used as the doer of the action (before the verb).
    • She reads a book.
    • They play football.
  • Object Pronoun: Used as the receiver of the action (after the verb or preposition).
    • I see her.
    • The teacher called them.
⚠️ Common Mistake:
❌ "Her is my friend." → ✅ "She is my friend."
❌ "Me and Budi went to school." → ✅ "Budi and I went to school."

E. Example Sentences

1. Nouns in Sentences

  • The teacher (common noun) teaches at MTsN 6 Agam (proper noun).
  • Happiness (abstract noun) comes from within.
  • I bought three books (countable noun) and some rice (uncountable noun).
  • A flock (collective noun) of birds flew over the school.

2. Pronouns in Sentences

  • She (subject) is my best friend. I like her (object) very much.
  • This is my pen. That is your pen.
  • I made the cake myself.
  • Who is the new student?
  • Everyone is happy today.

F. Example Dialogues

Dialogue 1: Using Pronouns in Conversation

Siti: Hi, Aisyah! Is this your book?

Aisyah: Yes, it's mine. Thank you for finding it.

Siti: You're welcome. Where is Budi?

Aisyah: He is in the library. I saw him there an hour ago.

Siti: Oh, I need to talk to him. Can you tell him to meet me?

Aisyah: Sure, I will tell him.

Dialogue 2: Identifying Nouns

Teacher: Students, please find the nouns in this sentence: "The students study English in the classroom."

Andi: The nouns are "students", "English", and "classroom".

Teacher: Very good! Which one is a proper noun?

Budi: "English" is a proper noun because it is the name of a subject.

Teacher: Excellent! And which ones are common nouns?

Andi: "Students" and "classroom" are common nouns.

Dialogue 3: Using Reflexive Pronouns

Rina: Did you make this cake yourself?

Dina: Yes, I made it myself. Do you like it?

Rina: It's delicious! You should be proud of yourself.

Dina: Thank you! I learned the recipe from my mother.

Rina: She must be a great cook too.

Dina: Yes, she is. She taught herself how to cook.

G. Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • ❌ Wrong: "Me and my friend went to school."
    ✓ Correct: "My friend and I went to school."
  • ❌ Wrong: "Him is my brother."
    ✓ Correct: "He is my brother."
  • ❌ Wrong: "This is mine book."
    ✓ Correct: "This is my book." or "This book is mine."
  • ❌ Wrong: "The cat hurt it self."
    ✓ Correct: "The cat hurt itself."
  • ❌ Wrong: "Everyone are happy."
    ✓ Correct: "Everyone is happy." (Everyone is singular)

H. Cultural Notes (Catatan Budaya)

  • Capitalization: In English, proper nouns (names of people, places, days, months) are always capitalized. This is very important in writing.
  • Gender-neutral language: Modern English increasingly uses "they" as a singular pronoun when the gender is unknown or non-binary.
  • Politeness: Using the correct pronoun shows respect. For example, using "he" or "she" instead of "it" when referring to people.
  • Countable vs Uncountable: Some nouns are countable in Indonesian but uncountable in English (e.g., information, advice, news, furniture).

📝 Poin Penting untuk Ujian (Key Points for the Test)

  • Pahami perbedaan antara common noun dan proper noun (proper noun selalu diawali huruf kapital).
  • Kuasai perbedaan concrete vs abstract, countable vs uncountable, dan collective nouns.
  • Hafalkan tabel personal pronouns (subject, object, possessive adjective, possessive pronoun).
  • Pahami penggunaan demonstrative pronouns (this, that, these, those) berdasarkan jarak dan jumlah.
  • Kuasai penggunaan reflexive pronouns (myself, yourself, himself, dll).
  • Pahami perbedaan subject pronoun dan object pronoun dalam kalimat.
  • Hafalkan interrogative pronouns (who, what, which, whom, whose) dan fungsinya.
  • Perhatikan kesalahan umum seperti "Me and my friend" → "My friend and I".
  • Ingat bahwa everyone, everybody, someone, nobody adalah singular (menggunakan kata kerja singular).
