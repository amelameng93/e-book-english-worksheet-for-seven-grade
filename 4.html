BAB 4

Introducing Self and Others

Learning how to introduce yourself and other people politely in various situations. Building confidence in starting new conversations and making friends.

🏠 Beranda 📝 Uji Formatif Bab 4 ← Bab Sebelumnya Bab Selanjutnya →

A. Introduction to Introducing

Introducing is the act of making yourself or another person known to someone else. It is the first step in building a relationship and starting a conversation.

There are two types of introducing:

  • Introducing Self: Telling others about yourself (your name, age, address, hobbies, etc.)
  • Introducing Others: Telling someone about another person (a friend, family member, teacher, etc.)

Introducing is important because it:

  • Helps people get to know each other
  • Shows politeness and good manners
  • Builds confidence in social situations
  • Opens the door for further conversation

B. Introducing Yourself

1. Formal Self-Introduction

Used in official situations, with teachers, older people, or people you don't know well:

  • Good morning. My name is...
  • Let me introduce myself. I am...
  • Allow me to introduce myself. My name is...
  • I'd like to introduce myself. I'm...
  • My name is... You can call me...

2. Informal Self-Introduction

Used with friends, classmates, or people of the same age:

  • Hi! I'm...
  • Hello! My name is...
  • Hey! I'm... Nice to meet you.
  • Hi there! I'm...

3. Personal Information to Include

When introducing yourself, you can mention:

Information Example
Name My name is Siti Aisyah.
Nickname You can call me Aisyah.
Age I am twelve years old.
Address I live in Bukittinggi.
School I study at MTsN 6 Agam.
Class I am in class 7A.
Hobby My hobby is reading.
Family I have one brother and one sister.

C. Introducing Others

1. Formal Introduction of Others

Used in formal situations or when introducing someone older or in a higher position:

  • May I introduce my friend, Andi?
  • I'd like to introduce you to my classmate, Budi.
  • This is my teacher, Mr. Ahmad.
  • Allow me to introduce my sister, Rina.
  • Let me introduce my cousin, Dina.

2. Informal Introduction of Others

Used with friends or people of the same age:

  • This is my friend, Andi.
  • Andi, this is my brother, Budi.
  • Have you met my friend, Siti?
  • I want you to meet my classmate, Aisyah.

3. Information to Include When Introducing Others

  • Name: This is my friend, Andi.
  • Relationship: He is my classmate.
  • Origin: She is from Padang.
  • School/Work: He studies at MTsN 6 Agam.

D. Responding to Introductions

When someone introduces themselves or introduces another person, it is polite to respond:

1. Formal Responses

  • Nice to meet you.
  • Pleased to meet you.
  • It's a pleasure to meet you.
  • How do you do? (Very formal, used only in very formal situations)
  • I'm honored to meet you.

2. Informal Responses

  • Nice to meet you too.
  • Glad to meet you.
  • Hi! Nice to meet you.
  • Hello! Good to see you.

3. Follow-up Questions

After the introduction, you can continue the conversation with:

  • Where are you from?
  • What school do you go to?
  • What are your hobbies?
  • How old are you?
  • Do you have any brothers or sisters?

E. Example Dialogues

Dialogue 1: Self-Introduction (Formal)

(First day of school, in the classroom)

Teacher: Good morning, students. Let's start with introductions. Please introduce yourself.

Siti: Good morning, Sir. Let me introduce myself. My name is Siti Aisyah. You can call me Siti. I am twelve years old. I live in Bukittinggi. I study at MTsN 6 Agam. My hobby is reading.

Teacher: Very good, Siti. Thank you. Nice to meet you.

Siti: Nice to meet you too, Sir.

Dialogue 2: Self-Introduction (Informal)

(Two students meet for the first time at the library)

Andi: Hi! I'm Andi. What's your name?

Budi: Hello! My name is Budi. Nice to meet you.

Andi: Nice to meet you too, Budi. Where are you from?

Budi: I'm from Agam. How about you?

Andi: I'm from Bukittinggi. What school do you go to?

Budi: I study at MTsN 6 Agam. And you?

Andi: Me too! We're classmates then!

Budi: That's great! Let's be friends.

Dialogue 3: Introducing Others

(Siti introduces her friend to her brother)

Siti: Budi, this is my friend, Aisyah. She is my classmate at MTsN 6 Agam.

Budi: Nice to meet you, Aisyah. I'm Budi, Siti's brother.

Aisyah: Nice to meet you too, Budi. Siti has told me about you.

Budi: Oh really? What did she say?

Aisyah: She said you are very kind and helpful.

Budi: Thank you. Where do you live, Aisyah?

Aisyah: I live near the school. It only takes 10 minutes to get there.

Budi: That's convenient. Well, I have to go now. Nice meeting you.

Aisyah: Nice meeting you too. See you around!

Dialogue 4: Introducing a Teacher

(A student introduces a new teacher to the class)

Student: Good morning, everyone. I'd like to introduce our new English teacher, Mrs. Rina.

Students: Good morning, Mrs. Rina.

Mrs. Rina: Good morning, students. I'm very happy to be here. Let me introduce myself. My name is Rina Amalia. You can call me Mrs. Rina. I graduated from UIN Bukittinggi. I love teaching English and I hope we can learn together.

Students: Welcome, Mrs. Rina!

F. Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • ❌ Wrong: "I am have twelve years old."
    ✓ Correct: "I am twelve years old." or "I am twelve."
  • ❌ Wrong: "My name is called Siti."
    ✓ Correct: "My name is Siti." or "I am called Siti."
  • ❌ Wrong: "I live at Bukittinggi."
    ✓ Correct: "I live in Bukittinggi."
  • ❌ Wrong: "Nice meet you."
    ✓ Correct: "Nice to meet you."

G. Cultural Notes (Catatan Budaya)

  • Handshake: In English-speaking cultures, people often shake hands when meeting for the first time. A firm handshake shows confidence.
  • Eye Contact: Looking into the other person's eyes during introduction shows that you are confident and respectful.
  • Smile: A smile is universal and makes you appear friendly and approachable.
  • First Name vs. Last Name: In Western cultures, people often use first names even in semi-formal situations. In formal situations, use Mr., Mrs., Miss, or Ms. + last name.
  • Personal Space: Respect the other person's personal space. Don't stand too close or too far away.
  • Politeness: Always use polite expressions like "please", "thank you", and "nice to meet you".

📝 Poin Penting untuk Ujian (Key Points for the Test)

  • Pahami perbedaan antara formal dan informal introductions.
  • Kuasai ungkapan untuk memperkenalkan diri sendiri (introducing self) dan orang lain (introducing others).
  • Hafalkan respons yang tepat terhadap perkenalan (nice to meet you, pleased to meet you, dll).
  • Kuasai kosakata untuk menyebutkan informasi pribadi: name, age, address, school, hobby, family.
  • Perhatikan penggunaan preposisi yang tepat: live in (kota/negara), live at (alamat spesifik).
  • Latihan membuat dialog perkenalan sederhana.
  • Pahami perbedaan antara "My name is..." dan "I am called...".
  • Kuasai pertanyaan lanjutan setelah perkenalan: "Where are you from?", "What school do you go to?"
