BAB 3

Telling Time and Date

Learning how to ask and tell time, as well as how to express dates in English. Mastering the vocabulary and expressions for daily time management.

🏠 Beranda 📝 Uji Formatif Bab 3 ← Bab Sebelumnya Bab Selanjutnya →

A. Introduction to Telling Time

Telling time is one of the most important skills in daily communication. We need to know the time for various activities such as going to school, meeting friends, watching TV, and many more.

In English, there are two main ways to tell time:

  • Official/24-hour format: Used in formal situations (e.g., train schedules, airports)
  • Informal/12-hour format: Used in daily conversations with AM (before noon) and PM (after noon)

B. Asking and Telling Time

1. Common Questions to Ask Time

  • What time is it?
  • What's the time?
  • Do you have the time?
  • Could you tell me the time, please?
  • Excuse me, what time is it?

2. Telling Time with "O'clock"

Use "o'clock" when the time is exactly on the hour:

  • 07:00 → It's seven o'clock.
  • 09:00 → It's nine o'clock.
  • 12:00 → It's twelve o'clock. (or noon/midnight)

3. Telling Time with Minutes

When the time is not exactly on the hour, we say the hour first, then the minutes:

  • 07:15 → It's seven fifteen.
  • 08:30 → It's eight thirty.
  • 09:45 → It's nine forty-five.

C. Using "Past" and "To"

1. Using "Past" (for minutes 1-30)

Use "past" when the minutes are between 1 and 30:

  • 07:10 → It's ten past seven.
  • 08:20 → It's twenty past eight.
  • 09:25 → It's twenty-five past nine.

2. Using "To" (for minutes 31-59)

Use "to" when the minutes are between 31 and 59. We count how many minutes are left until the next hour:

  • 07:40 → It's twenty to eight. (20 minutes left until 8:00)
  • 08:50 → It's ten to nine. (10 minutes left until 9:00)
  • 09:35 → It's twenty-five to ten. (25 minutes left until 10:00)

3. Special Expressions: "Quarter" and "Half"

  • 15 minutes = a quarter
    • 07:15 → It's a quarter past seven.
    • 08:45 → It's a quarter to nine.
  • 30 minutes = half past
    • 09:30 → It's half past nine.
    • 10:30 → It's half past ten.

D. AM and PM

In the 12-hour clock system, we use AM and PM to distinguish between morning/afternoon and evening/night:

  • AM (Ante Meridiem): From midnight (12:00 AM) to before noon (11:59 AM)
  • PM (Post Meridiem): From noon (12:00 PM) to before midnight (11:59 PM)
Time Expression Period

12:00 12 PM (noon) Noon
00:00 12 AM (midnight) Midnight

E. Days of the Week

There are seven days in a week:

  1. Monday (Senin)
  2. Tuesday (Selasa)
  3. Wednesday (Rabu)
  4. Thursday (Kamis)
  5. Friday (Jumat)
  6. Saturday (Sabtu)
  7. Sunday (Minggu)

Note: In English, days of the week always start with a capital letter.

F. Months of the Year

There are twelve months in a year:

  1. January (Januari)
  2. February (Februari)
  3. March (Maret)
  4. April (April)
  5. May (Mei)
  6. June (Juni)
  7. July (Juli)
  8. August (Agustus)
  9. September (September)
  10. October (Oktober)
  11. November (November)
  12. December (Desember)

Note: Months of the year also always start with a capital letter.

G. Telling Dates

1. How to Say Dates

There are two common ways to say dates in English:

  • Format 1: Month + Day (ordinal number)
    • June 25 → June twenty-fifth
    • January 1 → January first
    • December 31 → December thirty-first
  • Format 2: The + Day (ordinal number) + of + Month
    • June 25 → The twenty-fifth of June
    • January 1 → The first of January

2. Ordinal Numbers (1st - 31st)

  • 1st = first, 2nd = second, 3rd = third
  • 4th = fourth, 5th = fifth, 6th = sixth
  • 11th = eleventh, 12th = twelfth, 13th = thirteenth
  • 20th = twentieth, 21st = twenty-first, 22nd = twenty-second
  • 30th = thirtieth, 31st = thirty-first

3. Asking About Dates

  • What's the date today?
  • What date is it?
  • When is your birthday?
  • What day is it today? (asking about the day of the week)

H. Prepositions of Time: AT, ON, IN

1. AT - for specific times

  • at 7 o'clock
  • at noon
  • at midnight
  • at night

2. ON - for days and dates

  • on Monday
  • on June 25th
  • on my birthday
  • on Christmas Day

3. IN - for months, years, seasons, and parts of the day

  • in January
  • in 2026
  • in the morning
  • in the afternoon
  • in the evening

I. Example Dialogues

Dialogue 1: Asking Time

Andi: Excuse me, what time is it?

Budi: It's half past seven.

Andi: Oh, I'm late for school. Thank you!

Budi: You're welcome. Hurry up!

Dialogue 2: Talking About Schedule

Teacher: What time do you go to school?

Student: I go to school at a quarter to seven.

Teacher: And what time does the class start?

Student: The class starts at seven o'clock.

Teacher: Good. Don't be late.

Dialogue 3: Talking About Dates

Siti: When is your birthday, Aisyah?

Aisyah: My birthday is on August 17th.

Siti: Oh, that's Independence Day! What day is it this year?

Aisyah: It's on Monday.

Siti: That's great! We can celebrate together.

Dialogue 4: Making an Appointment

Rina: Are you free on Friday?

Dina: Yes, I am. Why?

Rina: Let's go to the library. What time do you want to meet?

Dina: How about at 3 PM?

Rina: Perfect! See you on Friday at 3 PM.

Dina: See you!

J. Cultural Notes (Catatan Budaya)

  • 24-hour vs 12-hour clock: In the UK and US, people commonly use the 12-hour clock in daily life. The 24-hour clock is used in official schedules (transportation, military).
  • Date format: In American English, the date is written as Month/Day/Year (e.g., 06/25/2026). In British English, it's Day/Month/Year (e.g., 25/06/2026).
  • Punctuality: In English-speaking cultures, being on time is very important. Being late is considered impolite.
  • Weekend: Saturday and Sunday are considered the weekend in most English-speaking countries.

📝 Poin Penting untuk Ujian (Key Points for the Test)

  • Kuasai cara menanyakan dan memberitahu waktu menggunakan "What time is it?"
  • Pahami penggunaan "o'clock", "past", "to", "quarter", dan "half"
  • Hafalkan hari-hari dalam seminggu (days of the week) dan bulan-bulan dalam setahun (months of the year)
  • Kuasai cara menyebutkan tanggal menggunakan ordinal numbers (1st, 2nd, 3rd, dll)
  • Pahami perbedaan penggunaan preposisi at, on, dan in untuk waktu
  • Latihan membuat dialog sederhana tentang waktu dan tanggal
  • Perhatikan perbedaan antara AM (pagi/siang) dan PM (sore/malam)
