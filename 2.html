BAB 2

Expressions of Thanking and Apologizing

Learning how to express gratitude and apologize politely in various situations. Building good manners and positive communication in English.

🏠 Beranda 📝 Uji Formatif Bab 2 ← Bab Sebelumnya Bab Selanjutnya →

A. Introduction to Thanking and Apologizing

Thanking is an expression used to show appreciation or gratitude to someone who has helped us, given us something, or done something kind for us.

Apologizing is an expression used to say sorry when we make a mistake, cause trouble, or hurt someone's feelings.

Both expressions are very important in daily communication because they show:

  • Politeness: Good manners in social interactions
  • Respect: Appreciation for other people
  • Responsibility: Willingness to admit mistakes
  • Good Relationship: Building positive connections with others

B. Expressions of Thanking

1. Formal Thanking

  • Thank you very much.
  • Thank you so much for your help.
  • I really appreciate your help.
  • I am very grateful to you.
  • Many thanks for your kindness.
  • I would like to express my gratitude.

2. Informal Thanking

  • Thanks!
  • Thanks a lot!
  • Thanks a million!
  • Cheers! (British English)
  • You're a lifesaver!
  • I owe you one.
  • That's very kind of you.

C. Responding to Thanking

When someone thanks you, it is polite to respond. Here are the common responses:

1. Formal Responses

  • You're welcome.
  • My pleasure. / It's my pleasure.
  • Don't mention it.
  • It was nothing.
  • I'm glad I could help.
  • Anytime.

2. Informal Responses

  • No problem.
  • No worries.
  • Sure thing.
  • Of course.
  • That's alright.

D. Expressions of Apologizing

1. Formal Apologizing

  • I apologize for being late.
  • I am very sorry for my mistake.
  • Please accept my sincere apology.
  • I beg your pardon.
  • I am terribly sorry.
  • Please forgive me.

2. Informal Apologizing

  • Sorry!
  • I'm sorry.
  • Sorry about that.
  • My bad.
  • Oops, sorry!
  • My apologies.

E. Responding to Apologizing

When someone apologizes to you, it is polite to accept their apology. Here are the common responses:

  • That's alright.
  • That's okay.
  • No problem.
  • Don't worry about it.
  • It doesn't matter.
  • Forget it.
  • Never mind.
  • I forgive you.

F. Example Dialogues

Dialogue 1: Thanking (At school)

Siti: Aisyah, may I borrow your pen?

Aisyah: Sure, here you are.

Siti: Thank you very much.

Aisyah: You're welcome.

(After class)

Siti: Here is your pen. Thanks a lot for lending it to me.

Aisyah: No problem. Anytime!

Dialogue 2: Apologizing (Between friends)

Budi: Good morning, Andi.

Andi: Good morning, Budi.

Budi: I'm sorry I couldn't come to your birthday party yesterday.

Andi: That's alright. What happened?

Budi: I was sick. I had a fever.

Andi: Oh, I'm sorry to hear that. Don't worry about it. Are you feeling better now?

Budi: Yes, much better. Thank you.

Dialogue 3: Thanking and Apologizing (Formal situation)

Student: Excuse me, Sir. I'm sorry for coming late.

Teacher: It's okay. What's the reason?

Student: I missed the bus, Sir. I apologize for the inconvenience.

Teacher: Don't worry about it. Please sit down.

Student: Thank you, Sir.

Teacher: You're welcome. Let's continue the lesson.

G. Cultural Notes (Catatan Budaya)

In English-speaking cultures, saying "thank you" and "sorry" is very common and expected in daily life:

  • Frequency: People say "thank you" and "sorry" much more often than in some other cultures.
  • Small Favors: Even for small things (like holding the door), people say "thank you".
  • Politeness: Saying "sorry" does not always mean you are wrong. It can simply show empathy or politeness.
  • Sincerity: When apologizing, it is important to sound sincere and mean what you say.

📝 Poin Penting untuk Ujian (Key Points for the Test)

  • Pahami perbedaan antara ungkapan formal dan informal untuk thanking dan apologizing.
  • Hafalkan berbagai cara merespons ucapan terima kasih dan permintaan maaf.
  • Perhatikan penggunaan ungkapan yang tepat sesuai konteks (sekolah, teman, guru).
  • Kuasai kosakata kunci: appreciate, grateful, apologize, forgive, pardon.
  • Latihan membuat dialog sederhana yang menggabungkan ungkapan thanking dan apologizing.
  • Pahami bahwa "I beg your pardon" bisa berarti meminta maaf ATAU meminta seseorang mengulangi ucapannya.
