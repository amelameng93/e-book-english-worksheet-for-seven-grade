BAB 1

Expressions of Greeting and Leave Taking

Learning how to greet and say goodbye in various situations, both formal and informal. Building confidence in daily English conversations.

A. Introduction to Greeting and Leave Taking

Greeting is an expression of welcoming someone when you meet them. It is the first step in building a conversation and showing politeness.

Leave Taking is an expression used when you part ways or end a conversation. It shows that you appreciate the time spent with the other person.

In English, greetings and leave takings are categorized based on the situation:

  • Formal: Used in official situations, with teachers, older people, or people you don't know well.
  • Informal: Used with friends, family, classmates, or people of the same age.

B. Expressions of Greeting

1. Formal Greetings

  • Good morning. (Used before 12:00 PM)
  • Good afternoon. (Used from 12:00 PM to 05:00 PM)
  • Good evening. (Used after 05:00 PM)
  • How do you do? (Used only when meeting someone for the first time in a formal setting)
  • How are you?
  • It's a pleasure to meet you.

2. Informal Greetings

  • Hi! / Hello!
  • Hey!
  • How's it going?
  • What's up?
  • How are you doing?
  • Long time no see. (Used when you haven't seen someone for a long time)

C. Responding to Greetings

When someone greets you, it is polite to respond. Here are the common responses:

  • Q: How are you?
    A: I'm fine, thank you. And you? / I'm doing great, thanks.
  • Q: How do you do?
    A: How do you do? (This is a special response. You reply with the exact same phrase!)
  • Q: What's up?
    A: Not much. / Nothing much.
  • Q: How's it going?
    A: It's going well. / Pretty good.

D. Expressions of Leave Taking

1. Formal Leave Taking

  • Goodbye.
  • It was nice meeting you.
  • Have a nice day.
  • See you tomorrow.

2. Informal Leave Taking

  • Bye!
  • See ya!
  • Catch you later.
  • I've got to go now.
  • Take care.

E. Example Dialogues

Dialogue 1: Formal (At school, meeting a teacher)

Student: Good morning, Sir.

Teacher: Good morning. How are you today?

Student: I'm fine, thank you. And how are you, Sir?

Teacher: I'm doing well, thank you.

(After the class is over)

Teacher: Well, class is over. Goodbye, everyone.

Students: Goodbye, Sir. Have a nice day.

Dialogue 2: Informal (Between two friends)

Andi: Hi, Budi! Long time no see.

Budi: Hi, Andi! Yeah, it's been a while. How's it going?

Andi: Pretty good. What about you?

Budi: Not bad. Hey, I've got to go now. See ya!

Andi: See ya! Take care.

F. Cultural Notes (Catatan Budaya)

In English-speaking cultures, body language is also a part of greeting:

  • Handshake: Common in formal situations. A firm handshake shows confidence.
  • Eye Contact: Looking into the other person's eyes shows that you are paying attention and are polite.
  • Smile: A smile is universal and always appreciated in both formal and informal situations.
  • Wave: Often used in informal situations when saying goodbye from a distance.

📝 Poin Penting untuk Ujian (Key Points for the Test)

  • Pahami perbedaan antara formal dan informal greetings/leave takings.
  • Hafalkan respons yang tepat untuk sapaan tertentu (contoh: "How do you do?" dijawab dengan "How do you do?").
  • Perhatikan penggunaan waktu untuk Good morning, Good afternoon, dan Good evening.
  • Kuasai kosakata perpisahan (leave taking) yang umum digunakan.
  • Latihan membuat dialog sederhana menggunakan ungkapan salam dan perpisahan.
